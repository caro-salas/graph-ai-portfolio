About Me

I am a Medical Research Technologist at the U.S. Naval Medical Research Unit South (NAMRU-S) with a passion for harnessing technology to improve global health. My research develops and validates molecular tools for malaria diagnosis and tracks drug resistance in Peru.

My laboratory expertise spans microscopy under CAP accreditation, Sanger sequencing, flow cytometry, and continuous Plasmodium falciparum cultures executed under BSL-2 standards and rigorous ethical practices.

International presentations have expanded my network across military and academic institutions in the Americas, Europe, Africa, and Asia, enabling rich collaborative projects. Most recently, I launched an initiative applying mathematical models to investigate the spread of antimalarial drug resistance in Loreto and inform evidence-based public-health decisions.
